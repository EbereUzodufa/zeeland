Most prominent real estate investors in Nigeria

In the last two decades, Forbes has listed 22 persons as richest men in the world, a feat they attained through their investment in real estate.

Moroccan property magnate Miloud Chaabi is Africa’s richest real estate tycoon, according to the 2015 Forbes Billionaires List. Chaabi is currently one of four African businessmen with significant interests in real estate worth an estimated $US one billion or more.

In Nigeria, the richest person in real estate is Aliko Dangote, whose net worth is $US 15.9 billion.

Although in Nigeria, there are no billionaires in dollars who have made their fortune solely on real estate yet. Notwithstanding, Nigeria investors are making their own fair share from the rapidly growing real estate industry.

On the list of the prominent Nigeria business magnates who are active in real estate industry is Aliko Dangote

A man that needs no introduction. A large part of Aliko Dangote’s wealth comes from his warehouses, luxury flats and commercial buildings in Lagos, Cross River, Kano, Rivers and Abuja. In addition to real estate, Dangote also manufactures exports and imports goods into Nigeria.

Folorunsho Alakija, Is Nigeria’s richest woman and one of the top 10 richest women in Africa. She is the group managing director of The Rose of Sharon Group which consists of The Rose of Sharon Prints & Promotions Limited and Digital Reality Prints Limited and the executive vice-chairman of Famfa Oil Limited, one of Nigeria’s biggest oil producers. The billionaire oil magnate once unseated Oprah Winfrey as the richest woman on earth as at 2014. With an estimated net worth of $1.65 billion as at January 2018, she was ranked by Forbes as the 2nd most powerful woman in Africa and 87th most powerful person in the world as of 2015.

What many do not know about Alakija is that she is a big player in the property and real estate sector in Nigeria. Having hit it big in the oil sector, she has invested a lot of her wealth in real estate. Among her numerous business investments is a real estate company called Dayspring Property Development Company Limited, which is currently developing her ongoing project, Rose of Sharon Towers. The building is located in Victoria Island, Lagos, with a magnificent view of Kuramo Waters and the Atlantic Ocean. There are three residential floors consisting 32 individual apartments of one, two and three bedrooms, all constructed to international standard and luxury finish.

Sources also revealed she is investing a minimum of N29 billion on another property project in the Federal Capital Territory.

Energetic, rich and relatively young, Oba Adeyeye Ogunwusi Enitan. He his the traditional monarch of Yoruba people of south western Nigeria. Though, he has just come into prominence as a result of his emergence to the throne. He was never a minor in the real estate industry. Through his company Gran Imperio Group he was able to take Lagos real estate by storm. With, over 13 years of experience. It won’t come as a surprise of Ile-Ife start experiencing a fast rising real estate development.

The list goes on, but this is just to capture a few of the wealthy Nigerians into the real estate industry.

Whatever might have drawn their interest to this booming industry, it can’t be unconnected to the goldmine, massive ROI, bankability, stability, little physical presence and the predictable nature of the real estate industry. These of course, are what every rich man wants from an investment.
